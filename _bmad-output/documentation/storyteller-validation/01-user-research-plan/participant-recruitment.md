# Participant Recruitment

## Target: 20 Participants

**Breakdown by Persona:**
- 7 participants matching "Sarah" (First-Time Author)
- 7 participants matching "James" (Prolific Pro)
- 6 participants matching "Elena" (Literary Perfectionist)

## Sarah (First-Time Author) - 7 Participants

**Screening Criteria:**
- Age 45-65
- Currently writing or aspiring to write first novel
- Low-to-moderate tech comfort (self-reported)
- Has tried writing software (Scrivener, Word, Google Docs, etc.)
- Frustrated by complexity or lack of AI assistance
- **Compensation:** $75 per 60-minute interview

**Recruitment Channels:**
- NaNoWriMo forums (post in "First-Time Participants" section)
- Reddit: r/writing, r/selfpublish, r/nanowrimo
- Local writing groups (Meetup.com)
- Facebook writing communities ("Aspiring Authors," "NaNoWriMo Community")
- Craigslist (writing/creative gigs section)

**Screening Questions:**
1. Are you currently writing or planning to write your first novel?
2. What writing tools have you tried? What frustrated you about them?
3. Have you tried AI writing tools? What was your experience?
4. On a scale of 1-10, how comfortable are you with technology?
5. What's the biggest challenge you face in writing your novel?

## James (Prolific Pro) - 7 Participants

**Screening Criteria:**
- Age 30-50
- Publishes 2+ novels per year (self-published or traditional)
- High tech comfort, uses multiple tools
- Productivity-focused, willing to pay for efficiency
- **Compensation:** $75 per 60-minute interview

**Recruitment Channels:**
- Indie author Twitter/X (search "#amwriting #indieauthor")
- KDP and self-publishing forums
- 20BooksTo50K Facebook group
- Prolific author podcasts (sponsor/advertise)
- LinkedIn (search "author" + high activity, 2+ books/year)

**Screening Questions:**
1. How many novels have you published in the last 12 months?
2. What writing and AI tools do you currently use?
3. What's your biggest productivity bottleneck?
4. How much do you currently spend per month on writing software/AI tools?
5. What would make you switch to a new writing tool?

## Elena (Literary Perfectionist) - 6 Participants

**Screening Criteria:**
- Age 25-40
- MFA or serious literary background
- AI-skeptical but curious
- Values craft, voice, control over speed
- Slower writing pace (1-2 years per novel)
- **Compensation:** $75 per 60-minute interview

**Recruitment Channels:**
- MFA program alumni networks (Iowa, Columbia, Michigan, etc.)
- Literary magazine contributor lists (Tin House, Granta, Paris Review)
- Writing workshop communities (Tin House, Bread Loaf, Sewanee)
- Literary agent Twitter/X followers
- Reddit: r/writing (literary fiction flair)

**Screening Questions:**
1. Do you have an MFA or equivalent literary training?
2. What's your approach to using AI in creative writing?
3. How long does it typically take you to complete a novel?
4. What do you value most: speed, voice, craft, or control?
5. What would make you trust an AI writing tool?
